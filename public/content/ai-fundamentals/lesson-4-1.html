AI Fundamentals: From Zero to Confident Making smart AI decisions

Claude vs. ChatGPT vs. Gemini — which one, when?

If you've been paralysed choosing between AI tools, this lesson ends the paralysis.

Every week, someone asks me which AI tool they should pay for. The honest answer is that it matters much less than the marketing would have you believe. All three top tools — Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini — are extraordinarily capable. Picking the "wrong" one won't hurt you. Picking none because you can't decide will.

This lesson is short, direct, and opinionated. You'll leave with a tool picked and a thirty-day plan to use it properly.

!
The common misconception

"I need to compare all three in detail before I can choose." You don't. The features overlap 80% of the time. The differences matter for specific jobs and personal taste — but only after you've actually used one for a while. Comparing in advance is a form of the tool-hunting from Lesson 3.3.

The three tools, honestly

Here's an unglamorous summary of what each one is currently best at. No rankings. No "winner." Just the fit.

Claude

From Anthropic

The one for writing

Produces the most natural-sounding prose of the three. Fewer clichés. Nuance when you ask for it. Long documents handled well.

  • Writing quality and voice
  • Long documents and reports
  • Honest about what it doesn't know
  • Strong at editing and tightening

Fit: anyone whose work is mostly written deliverables — consultants, copywriters, business owners, professionals. Pro tier: ~$20/month

ChatGPT

From OpenAI

The one for everything

The broadest ecosystem. Image generation built in. Voice mode. The deepest library of custom GPTs, plugins, and third-party integrations.

  • General-purpose versatility
  • Image generation (DALL·E)
  • Biggest ecosystem of add-ons
  • Strongest voice mode

Fit: anyone who wants one Swiss-army tool that covers writing, brainstorming, images, research, and voice. Plus tier: ~$20/month

Gemini

From Google

The one for Google Workspace

Lives inside Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Drive. If your work already lives in Google, it's the path of least friction.

  • Deep Google Workspace integration
  • Strong web search integration
  • Works directly inside Gmail and Docs
  • Reasonable price if already on Workspace

Fit: anyone who runs their business or work primarily inside Google Workspace and wants AI embedded in what they already use. Google AI Pro: ~$20/month

A note on the numbers. Pricing and features shift every few months. As of writing, the three main paid tiers sit at roughly $20/month each, with premium tiers available for heavier users. Treat the prices above as the current ballpark, not gospel — it's worth checking the latest when you decide to pay.

The take you're paying for

Here's where I'll stop hedging. Most people reading this are business owners, consultants, and professionals — not developers, not researchers. For that audience, my straight answer is this.

The recommendation

Start with Claude for writing tasks. Use ChatGPT for general, everyday AI. Ignore Gemini unless you live inside Google Workspace.

If you only pick one, pick Claude if your week is mostly writing, or ChatGPT if your week is mostly everything-else. Use it for thirty days. Then — only then — decide if you want a second.

Why Claude for writing? Because the output reads like a person wrote it rather than a machine. You'll do less editing, the voice sticks closer to what you gave it, and the model is noticeably less inclined to default to clichés. If the thing you produce most often is words that other humans will read, this matters.

Why ChatGPT for general? Because the ecosystem matters when you're not sure what you'll need next. Quick image, voice brainstorm, help with a spreadsheet, plain Q&A — it handles the lot, and the interface is familiar to everyone you'll talk to about AI. For the "one tool I can point anyone at" slot, it's the obvious default.

Why Gemini only if you're already a Google Workspace person? Because its advantage is specifically that — integration with things you already use. Outside that context, Claude and ChatGPT both pull ahead on general quality. Inside Workspace, drafting an email without switching tabs is a real productivity win.

The thirty-day rule

Here's the habit that will save you more time than any tool comparison. Once you've picked, use that tool — and only that tool — for thirty days. Properly. With the ROLE / CONTEXT / TASK pattern from Lesson 3.1, the trust hierarchy from 3.2, and the pre-flight check from 3.3.

What you'll find after thirty days is that you understand the tool deeply enough to know where it struggles for your work. That's when it's worth trying a second. Not before. Trying two tools at once, swapping constantly, learning neither — that's a month of tool-hunting in a new shirt.

If, after thirty days, you notice a consistent gap — your writing tool struggles with images, say, or your general-purpose tool produces stilted prose — add the second one. By then you'll know exactly what you're adding it for.

The one thing to remember

Pick one. Use it for 30 days. Then decide if you need a second.

The quality gap between "the best tool" and "a good tool used properly" is tiny. The gap between "no tool" and "one tool used properly" is enormous.

Two honest notes before you pick

On the free tiers. All three have usable free tiers. If you're still unsure, start free for a week and see which one you actually open. The one you reach for unprompted is your answer — far more informative than any feature comparison.

On what you won't notice. Most of the differences between these tools only matter at the margins, or on tasks none of them should be doing in the first place (like exact maths — see Lesson 1.3). For 90% of knowledge work, they're all genuinely good. Pick one, skip the comparison reviews, and get to using it properly.
